# IRB protocol — Knoll & van Dick (2013) silence-scale replication

This is a placeholder structure for the institutional review board (IRB)
submission required before any real-data collection (Phase A1–A5). The
actual approved protocol replaces this document once the IRB has signed off.

## Study overview

- **Title.** A multi-form replication of the Knoll & van Dick (2013)
  Four Forms of Employee Silence scale.
- **Risk level.** Minimal-risk (anonymous, low-stakes self-report survey).
- **Sample.** Adult employees recruited via Prolific UK/US (Phase A3,
  N ≈ 400). Optional Japanese sample via クラウドワークス / ランサーズ
  (Phase A5, N ≈ 200) for cross-cultural measurement-invariance work.
- **Compensation.** Prolific recommended rate ($9/hr; ≈ $1.50–$2.00 per
  8–12-minute completion).

## Informed consent

A consent form is shown at the start of the Qualtrics flow. Participants
must affirm consent before any survey items are presented. Wording is
plain-language, ≈ Grade 8 reading level, and covers:

- Study purpose, duration, and compensation.
- Voluntary participation and withdrawal at any point with no penalty.
- Confidentiality of responses; PII handling (Prolific ID → unlinked from
  responses post-completion).
- Use of aggregate-level results in publications, conference talks, and
  open-data repositories. **Raw rows are never shared.**
- Contact info for principal investigator and IRB office.

## Data protection

- **Raw data.** Encrypted at rest (AES-256) in `data_external/` (gitignored).
  Stored on a researcher-controlled drive with two-factor authentication;
  never on shared drives.
- **PII.** Prolific ID kept only long enough to verify completion and pay
  participants. After payment confirmation, Prolific ID is deleted; the
  remaining anonymous row is retained for analysis.
- **Demographics retained.** Age band, gender, tenure band — coarse-grained
  to prevent re-identification.
- **Open data.** Only aggregate-level outputs (M / SD, α profiles, r
  matrices, CFA fit indices) are published, never row-level data.

## Risks and benefits

- **Risks.** Minimal psychological discomfort from reflecting on silence
  motives in own workplace. Contact info for employee-assistance hotlines
  is provided after the survey.
- **Benefits.** Direct benefit to participant: contribution to a
  cross-cultural replication of an established silence-motive scale.
  Indirect: improved measurement of organisational silence dimensions.
